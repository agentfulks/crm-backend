## Send Cadence

### Daily Velocity

| Metric | Value | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Daily Sends | 8-12 messages | VC outreach is high-stakes; quality over quantity |
| Weekly Capacity | 40-60 messages | Allows for thoughtful execution |
| Total Timeline | 7-8 business days | 72 cards ÷ 10/day average |

### Weekly Distribution

| Day | Sends | Notes |
|-----|-------|-------|
| Monday | 10 | Start week strong, avoid Monday morning (inbox flood) |
| Tuesday | 12 | Peak engagement day |
| Wednesday | 12 | Peak engagement day |
| Thursday | 10 | Good response rates |
| Friday | 6-8 | Light day, wind down |
| Weekend | 0 | No VC sends on weekends |

### Optimal Send Times

**Best Times (Recipient Time Zone):**
- 9:00-10:00 AM - Morning triage time
- 2:00-3:00 PM - Post-lunch review
- Avoid: Before 8 AM, after 5 PM, weekends

**Time Zone Strategy:**
- EST VCs: Send 9:00 AM EST
- PST VCs: Send 9:00 AM PST (12:00 PM EST)
- Batch by time zone to optimize for each

## Prioritization Framework

### Tier 1: Must-Send First (Days 1-3)

**Criteria:**
- Seed/Series A stage match
- Active gamedev investment thesis
- Recent gaming investments (last 12 months)
- Warm intro available
- Mutual connection

**Expected Count:** 15-20 cards
**Send Window:** Days 1-3

### Tier 2: High-Priority (Days 4-6)

**Criteria:**
- Early-stage focus (pre-seed to A)
- Some gaming exposure
- Geographic proximity (easier meetings)
- Partner you've researched thoroughly
- Strong portfolio fit

**Expected Count:** 25-30 cards
**Send Window:** Days 4-6

### Tier 3: Strategic Fill (Days 7-8)

**Criteria:**
- Stretch stage fit (earlier/later than ideal)
- Less gaming focus but generalist interest
- Newer funds (hungrier)
- Corporate VCs with strategic fit

**Expected Count:** 20-27 cards
**Send Window:** Days 7-8

## Follow-up Cadence

### VC-Specific Follow-up Approach

VCs are different from BDR prospects:
- Higher baseline reply rate (25-35% vs 15-20%)
- Longer decision cycles
- More gatekeepers (analysts, associates)
- Different inbox management

### Touch 1: Initial Outreach (Day 0)
**Trello:** Move to "Sent - Awaiting Reply"
**Due Date:** Day 5 (VCs slower to respond)

### Touch 2: First Follow-up (Day 5-7)
**Trigger:** No reply after 5-7 days

**Template Framework:**
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [Name],

Quick bump on my note below about [one-line reminder].

I know you see a lot of deals. The specific angle here is
[unique insight about your traction/team/market].

Worth 15 minutes this week or next?

[Your name]
```

**Trello Update:**
- Change label: "Touch-2"
- Due date: Day 12

### Touch 3: Final Follow-up (Day 12-14)
**Trigger:** No reply after 12-14 days

**Template Framework:**
```
Subject: Should I close the loop on this?

Hi [Name],

I've reached out twice about [topic] and understand if it's
not a fit or timing is off.

Rather than keep following up, I'll assume you're passing.

Best of luck with your current portfolio, and feel free to
reach out if anything changes.

[Your name]
```

**Trello Update:**
- Move to "Nurture / Closed"
- Label: "No Reply - Closed"
- Note: Final follow-up sent, moved to nurture

## Response Handling for VCs

### Response Types & Actions

| Response | Action | Next Step |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| "Send deck" | Send within 24h | Move to "Materials Sent" |
| "Send data room" | Prepare + send | Move to "Due Diligence" |
| "Intro to [Partner]" | Thank + request intro | Move to "Warm Intro Requested" |
| "Not at this stage" | Ask for referral | Move to "Nurture - Later Stage" |
| "Not our thesis" | Ask why + referrals | Move to "Closed - Wrong Fit" |
| "Too early" | Ask for timing | Move to "Nurture - Too Early" |
| Meeting proposed | Accept ASAP | Move to "Meeting Booked" |

### VC Meeting Booking Priority

**Respond Within:**
- Cold VCs: 24 hours
- Warm intros: 4 hours
- Partner requests: 2 hours

**Calendar Strategy:**
- Offer 2-3 specific time slots
- Include Calendly link as backup
- Be flexible on time zones
- Suggest 30 minutes (not 15 - VCs expect longer)

## Warm Intro Strategy

### Prioritizing Warm Intros

**If you have warm intros available:**
1. Send warm intros first (Days 1-2)
2. Follow up with cold outreach (Days 2-8)
3. Use warm intro momentum in cold emails: "Just chatted with [Mutual Connection] about..."

### Requesting Warm Intros

**Template for Connector:**
```
Hi [Connector],

Hope you're well! I'm reaching out because we're starting
conversations with VCs and [Partner Name] at [Fund] seems
like a great fit given [specific reason].

Would you be comfortable making an intro? Happy to send
a forwardable email if that helps.

No worries at all if not - completely understand.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

## Deck & Materials Strategy

### When to Send Deck

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| They ask for deck | Send within 24h, follow up in 3 days |
| No ask, but engaged | Wait until meeting, use as follow-up |
| Cold send with deck | Lower response rate, but some prefer it |

### Follow-up After Deck Send

**Day 3:**
```

Wanted to make sure you received the deck. Happy to walk
through it on a quick call if easier.

Any initial questions or feedback?

[Your name]
```

**Day 7 (no reply):**
```
Hi [Name],

Quick bump - understand you're busy. Should I assume you're
passing, or is there a better time to reconnect?

[Your name]
```

## Competitive Dynamics

### Avoiding Cross-Fund Confusion

**Best Practice:**
- Don't email multiple partners at same fund simultaneously
- Wait 7-10 days before trying second partner at same fund
- If rejected by one partner, wait 3 months before another

### Creating Urgency (Authentically)

**Legitimate urgency triggers:**
- "We're starting conversations with a few select funds this week"
- "Just closed our first paying customer"
- "Hit [milestone] last week"
- "Other firms are asking about our timeline"

**Avoid:** Fake deadlines, artificial scarcity, pressure tactics
