**Strategic Positioning:**
We are positioning at the convergence of three major vectors:
1. **AI-generated content** requiring dynamic economic infrastructure
2. **Player-owned economies** enabled by blockchain rails
3. **Autonomous agents** participating in persistent game worlds

The narrative: Current game infrastructure was built for human-authored static content. The shift to AI-generated, player-owned, autonomous economies requires new middleware—a layer that doesn't exist yet.

## Key Themes

### 1. AI-Generated Content Economics
All five funds have made recent investments in AI content generation (Scenario, Inworld, D-ID, Hour One, Poolside). The positioning: as AI floods markets with infinite content, economic infrastructure that can dynamically price and absorb that content becomes essential.

### 2. Crypto-Native Gaming Infrastructure
Four of five funds (all except NFX) have explicit crypto-gaming investments. Remagine and 1kx are pure crypto-native. This validates the thesis that blockchain rails are necessary for player-owned economies.

### 3. B2B Infrastructure Over Content
Smok, Remagine, and 1kx explicitly prefer B2B infrastructure over B2C content plays. Moonfire and NFX have infrastructure bias. This positions our middleware play favorably against content studios.

### 4. Network Effects and Defensibility
NFX's core thesis on network effects aligns with our positioning: AI-driven economies create multi-sided network effects where players train models, models create value, and value attracts more players.

## Strategic Positioning

**For NFX:** Emphasize network effects flywheel and defensibility through platform dynamics. Reference portfolio companies (Voldex, Playable Worlds) to show thesis alignment.
