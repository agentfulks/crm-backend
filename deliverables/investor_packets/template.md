## Voice & Tone

**Do:**
- Write like a founder talking to another founder
- Be direct, specific, and concise
- Reference actual portfolio companies and thesis alignment
- Include concrete traction (LOIs, pilots, customers)
- Create urgency without hype

**Don't:**
- Use corporate fluff or buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage")
- Send generic flattery ("love what you're building")
- Include hype without proof ("game-changing", "revolutionary")
- Write more than 3 sentences in the opening hook
- Use passive voice ("We are seeking") — use active ("We're raising")

## Anti-Patterns: What NOT to Include

❌ **Generic flattery:** "I love what you're building at [Fund]"  
✅ **Specific thesis alignment:** "Your Gaming 3.0 thesis and investments in infrastructure like Solsten align with..."

❌ **Hype without proof:** "Revolutionary AI that will change gaming forever"  
✅ **Concrete traction:** "AI pipeline that reduces dev time by 60%, validated by 3 signed LOIs from mid-size studios"

❌ **Passive voice / corporate:** "We are seeking strategic partners to help us leverage..."  
✅ **Active voice / direct:** "We're raising Seed and looking for investors who understand gaming infrastructure"

❌ **Unclear CTA:** "Let me know if you'd like to learn more"  
✅ **Specific ask:** "Worth a 15-minute call to explore fit? We're closing the round in 4 weeks"
